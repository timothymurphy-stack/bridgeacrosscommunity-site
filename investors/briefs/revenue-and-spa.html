Deeper Revenue & Sales Promotions Agency (SPA)

Layered only when dignity is safe; designed for mutual benefit

Stance

SPA is **not** an advertising blast. It is a dignity-first promotions layer that arrives **after** the CSS habit is real and audited. The test: if turning it off harms dignity, we don’t turn it on.

Pre-conditions to switch on

  • Evidence first: CSS daily restorations stable; households see balances nightly.
  • Governance ready: privacy compact lived; consent pathways for any offer streams.
  • Retailer fit: partners agree to restoration-linked promotions (saver wins first).

What SPA actually does

  • Promotes saving actions (not spending) — e.g., “Save £x today, restoration at 23:30.”
  • Reward model aligns to saver benefit; BAC retains a transparent share for operations.
  • Local-first offers from participating retailers; opt-in, respectful frequency.

Channels & frequency (opt-in)

  • In-app / portal nudges tied to saver’s own cadence (never shaming).
  • SMS/email at low frequency; quiet periods respected; simple opt-out.
  • In-store prompts at participating retailers (no PII to floor staff).

Revenue model (illustrative)

  • Restoration-linked incentives: retailer contributes a small % tied to confirmed restoration.
  • Co-funded campaigns: community events where a day’s restorations are amplified.
  • Data-safe insights: aggregated, privacy-preserving trend summaries for partners.

Safeguards

  • Consent first: SPA only for households who opt-in.
  • Kill-switch: immediate disable if dignity is at risk or ratios degrade.
  • Board oversight: quarterly review; narrative checks; partner code of conduct.

Why this matters

When habit is established, SPA can accelerate restorations and fund local stewardship — without extracting from households. It keeps the system community-positive and replication-ready.

In one line: SPA is a dignity-first accelerator — only layered after CSS habit is proven, with benefits flowing to savers and community first.
