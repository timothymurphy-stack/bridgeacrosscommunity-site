Privacy Compact — Dignity & Governance

Consent that is real, data that is minimal, narratives that protect

Purpose

To ensure that every saver’s dignity is protected while enabling a community-owned savings fabric. This compact sets our stance on consent, data minimisation, narrative dignity, access, security, and accountability.

Consent (real, not implied)

  • Plain-language consent at onboarding; savers can ask questions and take time.
  • Revocation is simple — no penalty; support is offered if they choose to pause or exit.
  • Household-led choices on what is shared back (e.g., summary balances vs. narratives).

Data minimisation (enough to serve, no more)

  • Only what’s required to operate restoration loops, show balances, and audit flows.
  • Pseudonymised operational logs; personal identifiers stored separately where possible.
  • Access scoping: role-based restrictions for Chair, Steward, Practitioner.

Narrative dignity (people, not case studies)

  • No public exposure of households. Stories anonymised and consented.
  • Language audit: remove shaming terms; write with respect and accuracy.
  • Community review of public narratives before use.

Security & controls

  • Least privilege access; logs kept; periodic access reviews.
  • Encryption at rest and in transit for operational data; keys rotated.
  • Incident playbook: contain, notify, learn; timelines recorded; board briefed.

Retention & deletion

  • Operational data: retained only as long as needed for reconciliations and audits.
  • Right to be forgotten: honoured unless legal retention applies; clear process steps.
  • Backups: encrypted; deletion cascades to backups within a defined window.

Transparency to households

  • What we hold & why — provided on request in clear language.
  • How to correct — simple route to fix errors; confirmation when done.
  • How to complain — independent route; response time commitments.

Governance roles

  • Chair — accountable for stance; ensures compact is lived, not just written.
  • Steward — owns consent records, privacy logs, and narrative approvals.
  • Practitioner — frontline dignity; ensures households understand choices.

Compact in practice (Cornwall first)

  • Daily restorations with minimal data; nightly batch receipts retained.
  • Monthly privacy review (Chair–Steward–Practitioner) with action notes.
  • Quarterly board summary — redacted, aggregated, respectful.

In one line: We collect the least, protect the most, and tell stories that lift people — never expose them.
