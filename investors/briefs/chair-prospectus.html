Chair Prospectus — Role & OKRs

The Chair arrives at the level of the first saver — the first family — quietly, with fierce intent to see them begin. The act is authorship; the cadence is restoration.

Private — invitation only

Role & Presence

The Chair is not above the community but with it — present at Saver One. The first duty is to make starting possible: consent, safeguards, and a first advance that can be restored. Eyes stay on the family, not the theatre.

Operating Principles

  • Dignity by design: never-spent corpus; restoration beats rhetoric.
  • Small to prove, then repeat: Community ONE by stealth → a few more → regional; national later.
  • Transparency without spectacle: quiet reporting cadence; independent review.
  • Safeguards before scale: eligibility, verification, privacy compact.

180-Day OKRs (First Light)

  • O1: Author Saver One’s start.
    KR1.1 Consent captured; safeguards verified.
    KR1.2 First advance placed; restoration schedule co-agreed.
    KR1.3 First restoration loop completed and logged.
  • O2: Establish cadence & credibility.
    KR2.1 Weekly review rhythm (Chair + steward + practitioner).
    KR2.2 10 households oriented; 5 begin; 3 complete first loop.
  • O3: Safeguard the corpus & privacy.
    KR3.1 Governance & Privacy Compact adopted.
    KR3.2 Independent oversight engaged.
  • O4: Prepare the template for replication.
    KR4.1 Community ONE playbook v1 (internal).
    KR4.2 Two additional communities pre-qualified.

Cadence & Governance

  • Weekly: Chair + steward + practitioner review: consents, placements, restorations, issues.
  • Monthly: Quiet internal update: restoration rate, issues resolved.
  • Quarterly: Oversight review; privacy compact audit ticks.

Measures of Proof

  • Saver One begins; first loop restored on time.
  • Early cohort: 10 → 50 → 100 households with improving cadence.
  • Corpus intact; privacy honoured; spectacle refused.
