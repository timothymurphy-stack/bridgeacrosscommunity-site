Investor Positioning Brief

Answering a two-decade call — with a structural invention

The call

From Ron Sandler (2001) to Justin Welby and beyond, leaders have asked for innovation that genuinely reaches the financially excluded. Many initiatives helped, but the infrastructure to turn everyday discretion into durable savings — with dignity — has remained missing.

The answer

Community Savings Solution (CSS) is a real-time savings fabric that captures everyday amounts at the moment of choice (pennies to pounds), and restores a perpetual fund that is not spent, held in trust, managed for community. Bridge Across Community (BAC) provides distribution and human onboarding — beginning with Community One in Cornwall.

The breakthrough is a system of restoration loops: savers see their balances by day’s end; the charity fund restores on schedule; retailers settle on their normal cycles. The result is a habit engine with evidence visible early.

Why now

  • Real-time rails (payments + messaging) can support micro-restorations reliably.
  • Community partners (credit unions, local stewards) are ready to anchor trust.
  • Quiet replication from Cornwall outward can prove model before scale.

Positioning

  • Not another budgeting app; a structural layer for discretionary saving.
  • Not charity handouts; a perpetual community asset that is restored, not spent.
  • Not national first; Community One first, then a handful, then regional.

What we ask of the Chair / Investor

  • Stand at Saver 1: light the first household — quietly, with fierce intent.
  • Hold OKRs: 1 → 500 in finite steps, audited, humane.
  • Protect dignity: privacy compact respected; narratives told with care.

What you get

  • Early evidence: daily restorations; balances visible nightly.
  • Replicable system: steward + practitioner cadence; retailer batch; board-ready metrics.
  • Upside path: data-safe Sales Promotions Agency layered carefully, dignity-first.

In one line: CSS turns everyday discretion into durable savings, and BAC brings it to life — one family at a time — starting in Cornwall.
