CSS Strategic Brief

Real-time discretionary savings — daily habit, daily evidence

Design intent

CSS (Community Savings Solution) captures small, discretionary amounts in real time and restores a perpetual, safeguarded fund. Balances are visible to savers by end of day; retailers settle on their standard cycles; the charity fund restores on schedule — with dignity.

Flow of a day

  • Morning: Saver places a small amount; it is made available to their savings account.
  • Evening: Another small amount captured; end-of-day the two are reflected in their balance.
  • Nightly: The system posts a daily restoration using the retailer batch file.

The saver experiences convenient, dignified accumulation. The community fund experiences disciplined restoration. Both see progress each day.

Cadence & ratios

  • Short intervals early: daily restorations to establish habit and produce evidence quickly.
  • Operational reliability: retailer batch captured nightly; anomalies flagged for steward review.
  • Ratios improve with habit: restoration latency trends down; weekly variance stabilises.

People & roles

  • Chair: stands at Saver 1, holds the cadence, protects dignity and narrative.
  • Steward: consent, privacy compact, household baselines; monitors daily evidence.
  • Practitioner: weekly rhythm with families; supports the first restoration loops.

Governance

  • Privacy compact: consent is real; data is minimised; narratives are human.
  • Audit trail: restoration logs, cadence records, retailer batch receipts.
  • Perpetual fund: not spent; restored predictably; transparently governed.

Replication path

  • Cornwall — Community One: establish habit, cadence, and evidence quietly.
  • A few more communities: same pattern, same governance, same dignity.
  • Then regional: once 1 → 500 is proven and audited.

In one line: CSS makes saving as easy and frequent as spending — and proves it daily.
