CSS Strategic Brief

A never-spent savings architecture that restores cadence and dignity, beginning with Community ONE.

Private — invitation only

Core Premise

CSS — Community Savings Solution treats accumulation as first-class infrastructure. Funds are placed (advanced) but not consumed; they return via co-agreed restoration. This proves the model at human scale: one family → a cohort → a community.

Safeguarded Fund (Never-Spent)

  • Capital sits in a safeguarded structure with clear purpose & restrictions (never spent; only placed and restored).
  • Placement rules: small, deliberate, verified eligibility; first loop is tightly coached.
  • Restoration: schedule is co-agreed and humanly possible; early loops are short to build cadence.

Eligibility & Verification

  • Eligibility signals: residency in Community ONE, basic documentation, willingness to enter a restoration schedule.
  • Verification: practitioner-led (church/employer/CU) to keep it human, not bureaucratic.
  • Safeguards: privacy by default; minimal data; clear consent; independent oversight.

Placements & Restoration Ratios

  • Advance sizing: “small to prove, then repeat.” Start low; increase only after observed cadence.
  • Ratios: early loops target short intervals (e.g., weekly/fortnightly) to establish habit and evidence.
  • Slippage handling: pause, re-plan, reduce size; dignity first, theatre never.

Cadence & Reporting

  • Weekly: Chair + steward + practitioner review a one-page view: new consents, placements, restorations, issues.
  • Monthly: publish a quiet internal update: households in cadence, restoration rate, issues resolved.
  • Quarterly: oversight review and sign-off; privacy compact audit ticks.

Community ONE → Replication

Community ONE is the lighthouse. We refine quietly; then add a few more communities; then regional; national later. Cornwall remains the template. The pattern is consent → placement → restoration → cadence → habit → replication.

Interfaces

  • Churches & employers: trusted on-ramps; referral + pastoral support.
  • Credit Union / financial partner: placement & restoration rails; safeguarding.
  • BAC: the bridge — onboarding, communication, and replication playbook.

Bridge Across Community · CSS is the infrastructure; BAC is its bridge into community.
