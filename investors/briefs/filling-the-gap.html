“Filling the Gap” — Flagship

From a 20-year call to a working answer

The gap

For two decades, respected voices have called for innovation that reaches the financially excluded. Yet the structural ability to turn everyday discretionary choice into durable savings — reliably, with dignity — has remained missing. We call this the gap.

  • Help has often arrived as advice tools or credit alternatives — useful, but not a savings fabric.
  • Charitable flows are generous, yet typically spent, not restored into a perpetual asset.
  • Community partners exist, but lack rails to capture small, real-time intent to save.

The flagship answer

CSS (Community Savings Solution) is a real-time savings fabric that captures discretionary amounts — pennies to pounds — and restores a fund that is not spent, held in trust, managed for the community. Bridge Across Community (BAC) is the human distribution layer: the stories, the stewardship, the quiet onboarding of households.

The mechanism is a series of restoration loops. Savers see their balances by the end of each day; the fund restores on schedule; retailers settle on their normal cycles. Early habit + early evidence.

How it feels in real life

Monday morning: a mother saves a small amount on the school run; that amount is made available to her savings account. Monday evening: another amount at the grocer; balances reflect both by night. The saver experiences convenient, dignified accumulation — not depletion.

Why Cornwall first

  • Community One allows us to prove the cadence: chair + steward + practitioner.
  • Local partners (e.g., credit unions, retailers) can anchor trust and batch integrations.
  • Replication proceeds quietly: a few more communities, then regional — not national first.

What changes with this flagship

  • From advice → infrastructure: a layer that turns intent into savings, automatically.
  • From spend → restore: charitable capital becomes a perpetual, safeguarded asset.
  • From pilots → cadence: finite milestones (1 → 500) with daily, auditable evidence.

In one line: We don’t ask people to “be better” — we give them rails where small, real-time choices quietly add up, every day.
