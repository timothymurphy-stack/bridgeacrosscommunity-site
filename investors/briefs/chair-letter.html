Letter to the Chairperson

Standing at the level of Saver 1 — quiet intent, finite milestones

Dear Chair,

Thank you for stepping into this private folio. What follows is not a pitch, but an invitation to stand at the level of the first household in Cornwall — to light the first saver, and to shepherd a finite progression from 1 → 500. Your role is to bring clarity, courage, and cadence to a system designed for dignity.

What we are building

A real-time discretionary savings fabric (CSS) that captures everyday amounts — pennies to pounds — and restores a perpetual fund that is not spent, held in trust, and managed for the community. Bridge Across Community (BAC) provides the distribution and human onboarding, one household at a time.

This answers a two-decade call for inclusion — from Sandler to Welby — with a structural innovation: habit-forming restoration loops, daily visibility of balances, and stewarded replication, Cornwall first.

Your stance and OKRs

  • O1 — First Light: Light the first household quietly, with fierce intent. Protect their dignity.
  • KR1.1 Consent and privacy compact acknowledged; baseline captured.
  • KR1.2 First advance placed; restoration cadence co-agreed (daily, initially).
  • KR1.3 First restoration loop completed and logged.
  • O2 — Evidence: Establish a pattern across ten, then fifty savers. Ratios improve; cadence stabilises.
  • O3 — Stewarded Scale: Reach 500 with audit-ready metrics and a replication pack for the next communities.

How we begin — and why it matters

We begin with one family. Small, real-time amounts are captured as they live — a few pounds on a Monday morning, another in the evening — and balances are visible by night. The act of saving becomes convenient, dignified, and cumulative.

Your presence signals that this is not another pilot, but the careful ignition of a permanent, community-owned capability.

If this resonates, we propose a quiet session to confirm the first light plan and agree the initial cadence. From there, the Milestones ladder (1 → 500) will carry us.

With respect,

Bridge Across Community
